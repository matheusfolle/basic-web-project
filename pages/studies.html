“commit to the Lord whatever you do, and he will establish your plans.” — proverbs 16:3

the future will be like that — or even better

for a long time, I was buried under studies and goals, not realizing the deeper rhythm of what God was doing in my life. now, things are changing. in Deuteronomy 6:4-5, we find the foundation of Israel's faith:

“hear, oh Israel: the Lord our God, the Lord is one. love the Lord your God with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your strength.”

this verse, known as the shema (שְׁמַע), is not just a call to believe — it's a call to listen, obey, and live. it's a rhythm passed across generations. and what amazes me is how Jesus himself echoes it in Mark 12:28-34, when he says:

“the most important one,” answered Jesus, “is this: ‘hear, o israel: the Lord our God, the Lord is one...’”

that moment links something ancient and something new. the hebrew word shema (שְׁמַע) means more than hearing — it means listening with action. in greek, Jesus uses akouo (ἀκούω), a word that only appears once in this exact moment. that small difference carries a weight: it's not just about sound, it's about attention, response, and transformation.

I believe this same rhythm can echo through code, through design, through life... I no longer see my skills as separate from my purpose. ok, I want peace. a good home in a good neighborhood. I want stability. but I also want to build things that matter. I believe I can be part of something greater — to understand the world, speak its language, and use that awareness to improve people’s lives.

thanks be to God — because now, I'm able to see things more deeply and I want to stay that way.
